# Olist E-Commerce: Operations, Satisfaction & Payment Analysis

## Overview
An end-to-end Power BI analysis of the Olist Brazilian E-Commerce dataset, 
covering ~99K orders across order fulfillment, customer satisfaction, 
product performance, and payment behavior. The project combines interactive 
dashboards with a written report to surface where the business is losing 
revenue and satisfaction — and what to do about it.

## Key Results
- **99K** total orders analyzed | **$8.57M** revenue generated
- **97.02%** order success rate
- **4.09** average review score
- **9.02 days** average delivery time (vs. 2.66 days promised)

## Dashboard Pages
1. **Order Fulfillment** — delivery performance, order status breakdown, 
   and delivery-time trends from 2016–2018
2. **Customer Satisfaction & Complaints** — revenue trends, top product 
   categories, complaint drivers, and review score analysis
3. **Payment Method Analysis & Strategic Summary** — payment method 
   distribution, installment behavior, and seller-city delivery performance

## Key Insights
- Average delivery time (9.02 days) significantly exceeds the shipping 
  estimate (2.66 days), pointing to a fulfillment gap rather than a 
  quality issue
- 68%+ of complaints trace back to late/missing deliveries, not product 
  defects — and these categories also have the lowest review scores 
  (1.28–1.58)
- Credit cards drive 75K of all transactions and carry nearly all 
  installment financing (3.53 installments/order vs. 2.92 platform average)
- Delivery time is highly seller-city dependent: São Paulo (8.5 days) vs. 
  Boa Vista and Macapá (~27 days)

## Recommendations
- Prioritize logistics partnerships/local warehousing in high-delay cities
- Launch proactive customer service outreach on delayed orders before 
  reviews are submitted
- Focus inventory and promotions on top categories (Bed & Bath, 
  Health & Beauty, Sports & Leisure)
- Incentivize higher installment brackets on credit-card orders
